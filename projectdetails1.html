Tesco Grocery Redesign

A Next-Gen User Experience for 1.2 Million Weekly Users

Introduction

The Tesco Grocery Redesign project was initiated to modernize the digital shopping experience for one of the UK’s leading retailers. By focusing on a "Zero-Friction" design philosophy, the project aims to minimize cognitive load, ensuring the journey from product discovery to checkout is intuitive and efficient.

Our Approach

We followed a structured four-stage development lifecycle to identify pain points early and build a solution that is both scalable and highly performant.

  • Discovery: Identifying friction points where users abandoned carts.
  • Competitor Benchmarking: Analyzing industry leaders to set a high standard for UI/UX.

Information Architecture

We restructured the site’s hierarchy to prioritize speed, ensuring essential actions like "Search" and "Checkout" are always within reach.

  • Wireframing: Creating blueprints to map flow without distractions.
  • Prototyping: Building Figma models to test the "One-Tap" checkout logic.

Technical Implementation

Using Modular CSS (Grid/Flexbox) and a component-based architecture, we ensured visual consistency and rapid page load times.
